Absolutely Why You Should Attend Techno KreatiCon 3.0

Techno KreatiCon Phase 1 & 2, held at PPG Institute of Technology, Coimbatore, was a vibrant platform where young innovators pitched groundbreaking ideas addressing real‑world challenges. Projects were judged by industry experts on innovation, feasibility, and market potential, with live updates ensuring transparency. The event inspired students to push technological boundaries.

CyberZen, a student‑driven community at PPGIT, fosters innovation, technical expertise, and cybersecurity awareness. By organising events like Techno KreatiCon, along with workshops, hackathons, and competitions in coding, AI, and ethical hacking, the club bridges academia and industry, empowering students to thrive in the digital era.

About the Event

Techno KreatiCon 3.0 is a three-day national-level competition organised by the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at PPG Institute of Technology. This flagship event integrates two parallel tracks: Vibe Coding (technical knowledge, creativity, coding, and web deployment) and the Innovation Ideathon (ideate, innovate, and pitch practical solutions to real-world problems). Both tracks foster creativity, design thinking, problem-solving, and innovation, culminating in a grand finale before expert judges.

Rulebook & Guidelines

Event Structure

  1. Day 1 • Online Submit your website (Vibe Coding) or PPT (Ideathon) for evaluation. Results updated live.
  2. Day 2 • Online Theme/problem announced. Build and submit within the given time.
  3. Day 3 • On Campus Final challenge, live presentations, and a hands‑on workshop. Physical presence required for certificates and rewards.
  4. Judging Real‑time scorecard. Jury decisions are final and binding.

General Rules

  • Registration fee: ₹200 per participant.
  • Team size: 2–5 members.
  • Register via QR code or official link.
  • Open to all college students (UG/PG).
  • All work must be original. Plagiarism or copying leads to disqualification.
  • Respect deadlines, event etiquette, and instructions from organizers.
  • Misconduct, rule violations, or disruptive behavior will result in disqualification.

Code of Conduct

  • Online: Be respectful, keep your video on, and avoid disruptive communication.
  • On Campus: Be punctual, respect everyone, maintain discipline, and encourage healthy competition.
  • Any form of harassment or discrimination will not be tolerated.

Vibe Coding Track

  • Submit only self‑developed concepts; copied templates are strictly prohibited.
  • Pre‑built templates allowed only with significant custom code and documentation.
  • Follow best practices: clear structure, clean code, documentation, and responsive UI.
  • Submit source code, deployment link, and documentation before the deadline.
  • Judging criteria: Innovation, technical quality, usability, and presentation.

Innovation Ideathon Track

  • Use the official presentation template provided by organizers.
  • Abstract must clearly state: Problem, Solution, Use Case, and Impact.
  • Deck should be professional, concise, and within the allocated time.
  • Final live presentation with jury Q&A. Be prepared for questions.
  • Mockups, prototypes, and process flows are highly encouraged.
  • Judging criteria: Clarity, innovation, feasibility, and impact.
